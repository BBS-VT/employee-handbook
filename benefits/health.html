Health

We realize that every employee is unique, so we've opted to provide all full time employees with what's known as a health reimbursement arrangement (HRA). This type of health benefit is highly flexible and financially advantageous for all parties. Plus, it doesn't leave employees without coverage if their employment ends.

Why an HRA?

HRAs are consumer directed, meaning you have complete freedom to choose the coverage you need. You're generally required to have a medical plan, but you're free to choose the perfect option with the right combination of deductibles, coverage, premiums, etc. You can also supplement that with dental, vision, or prescription insurance plans according to your individual needs.

In addition, the monthly reimbursement allowance can be used to cover a variety of out-of-pocket expenses, like coinsurance, copayments, deductibles, shots, eyeglasses, and quite a bit more.

How it works

Qualified Small Employer Health Reimbursement Arrangements were enacted by Congress via the 21st Century Cures Act, and the regulations established there govern much of how our company's HRA works. Below are the monthly reimbursement limits we've elected to provide to every full time employee:

Classification Monthly limit
Single employee (no spouse or dependents) $400/mo
Employee with family (has spouse and/or dependents) $800/mo
