Personal Time Off

Personal time off is a flexible type of paid leave that can be used for just about any reason, besides vacations. It isn't earned, nor does it accrue; rather, it's given at the beginning of each year (or at the employee's start date if new). Here just a few examples of what to use personal time off for:

  • Sickness, disease, illness, etc.
  • A dependent is sick, needs to go to the doctor, etc.
  • Appointment
  • Jury duty
  • Bereavement
How it works

Every employee will receive 3 days of paid personal time off each year, at the start of the year. Newly hired employees will receive the same number of days off, but on their start date. Unused days will not roll over from year-to-year.

How to use

Personal time off can be used by notifying the person you report to as early as reasonably possible and gaining approval. (We realize that certain situations don't arise until the day of, like illness, which is okay.) Time off can be taken in whole-hour increments.
